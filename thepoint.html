Radiocarbon Dating
Priscilla Short

Overview

The Main Point

The scientific community has heavily relied on radiocarbon dating
since its discovery in the 1940s. As the inconsistencies and flaws of this
method are revealed, it is important to adjust and open to door to other dating
methods that may yield more accurate results. One important concept that can be
learned from the flaws seen in radiocarbon dating is that there are many unknown
factors in the dating method. Each factor can change the result of the dating process
by a large percentage. Drastic fluctuations have been seen in values that were
previously supposed to be constant values. Therefore, it is not inconceivable,
and could even be expected, that these fluctuations have taken place in a greater
degree in the past. This would mean that the dates that are given via
radiocarbon dating today could be more questionable than scientists
suppose. It is more important than ever to analyze the
radiocarbon dating method and discover ways to
check its accuracy.
