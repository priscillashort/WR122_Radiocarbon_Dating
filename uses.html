How Radiocarbon
Dating is Used

It is important to examine the fields that use radiocarbon dating because it
is important to know what scientific fields could be threatened when inconsistencies
and flaws are discovered in radiocarbon dating. The main fields that heavily use
radiocarbon dating are Archaeology and to a lesser degree, Geology. However, there
are several other ways that radiocarbon dating is being used, including dating of
significant artifacts like the Dead Sea Scrolls or the Shroud of Turin. These artifacts
and their supposed age can impact people who use the artifacts as a focal point
for what they believe and how they view the world. In this way, it is not
just the scientific fields that are threatened by using a flawed dating
method, but also the worldview and beliefs of many people.
