Potential Improvement

There may be a way that scientists can maintain the use of radiocarbon dating in
the scientific fields, while avoiding some of the flaws of the method. This compromise
could pave the way for other dating methods and other scientific discoveries in the future.
Scientists could use a method to check if radiocarbon dating could be accurately used on a specific
organic sample. When it is discovered that radiocarbon dating would not by an accurate dating method
for the sample, other dating methods could be used. Both the scientists developing the other dating
methods, as well as the scientific community in general would benefit from this solution. The scientific
community would benefit because the dates of the artifacts would be more accurate and certain
scientific fields would not be placing so much emphasis on one dating method.
