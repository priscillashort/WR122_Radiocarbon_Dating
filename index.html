Radiocarbon Dating
Priscilla Short


Overview


Radiocarbon dating is used as a primary method for attempting to date
organic material. Because this method is used as a foundation for several theories
concerning the age of the earth and artifacts, the method should be thoroughly analyzed. After
analyzing radiocarbon dating, scientists have found several inconsistencies and flaws that warrant
extensive research. Some of the flaws of radiocarbon dating include, the fluctuating amount of
Carbon-14 in the atmosphere over time, different levels of Carbon-14 in the atmosphere in different
regions, and the false assumption that the half-life of Carbon-14 is a constant value. Scientists should further
evaluate radiocarbon dating with a critical eye to determine if radiocarbon dating is reliable for accurately
dating organic material. If other methods can be found that are more accurate dating methods, than these
methods should be used instead of, or in conjunction with radiocarbon dating. A recent method that was
devised to check the accuracy of radiocarbon dating on a case by case basis could provide some
correction and calibration of the radiocarbon dating method and open the door to
other dating methods.
