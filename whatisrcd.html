What is
Radiocarbon Dating?

Radio carbon dating is a method of dating organic material that uses Carbon
isotopes and decay rates to try to determine the age of a meterial. Scientists in the
fields of Archaeology and Geology regard radiocarbon dating as unerring and
completely reliable for dating any organic material. Scientists have built the
radiocarbon dating method on several assumptions that have not changed since
the conception of radiocarbon dating in the 1940s. Radiocarbon dating is built
on the idea that the half-life of Carbon-14 is a constant value, the ratio of
Carbon isotopes in the atmosphere is constant over time, and the
environment does not impact these key values very much, or at all.
These concepts form the basis of the radiocarbon dating
method as it is known and used today.
