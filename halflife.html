Half-Life Fluctuation
Alex Knapp with Forbes Magazine reveals new breakthroughs in science
concerning the half-life of radioactive isotopes. Knapp explains that scientists use
the half-lives of radioactive isotopes as constant values in mathematical formulas to try to
calculate the age of a material. Knapp points out that the concept of a constant half-life is one of
the first ideas that a scientist learns when studying radiocarbon dating, because this concept forms the
basis of the method. However, Knapp also explains that scientists at Stanford and Purdue have discovered
that this supposedly constant half-life can vary. Knapp claims that, “if physicists at Stanford and Purdue are
correct in their findings, the whole theory of constant radioactive decay rates could be thrown out the door”
(Knapp). The scientists discovered this discrepancy in the half-life by trying to create a better way to generate
random numbers. When generating a random number a constant value and an unpredictable value is needed.
When scientists tried to use the half-life as the supposed constant value, they discovered that the value
fluctuated. Knapp elaborates by explaining that the half-life of a radioactive material can be affected
by solar neutrinos. However, the limited knowledge concerning solar neutrinos means that
scientists have very limited knowledge about why the half-life of radioactive materials
varies. Overall, scientists at Stanford and Purdue have discovered that the half-life
of radioactive materials is not a constant, revealing a major flaw in
radiocarbon dating, but they are unable to determine
why. (See Source Here)
