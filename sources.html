Cohn, Andrea. “Radiocarbon Dating: A Closer Look At Its Main Flaws.”
  Great Discoveries in Archaeology, 7 Feb. 2013, http://anthropology.msu.edu/
  anp264-ss13/2013/02/07/radiocarbon-dating-a-closer-look-at-its-main-flaws/.
Cohn is a student at MSU studying Archaeology. This article illustrates the way that radiocarbon dating is being presented to college students. The fact that Cohn is able to identify flaws in the radiocarbon dating method as a student but is not presented with solutions illustrates the shortcomings of radiocarbon dating in academia. This article illustrates an important aspect of how radiocarbon dating is used, not only in scientific fields, but also in academia. The radiocarbon dating method is being taught to college students as a primary dating method for determining accurate dates for organic material, and yet the students are able to identify inconsistencies of the method. Either these inconsistencies are presented by the professors or they are recognized readily by the students. However, the students are not provided with solutions to these flaws in radiocarbon dating. (Go to top)
